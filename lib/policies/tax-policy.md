## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for tax treatment, compliance, and administration related to international interconnect and roaming telecommunications services provided under Master Interconnect Agreements.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All international interconnect and roaming services
- Cross-border telecommunications transactions
- Partner invoicing and revenue recognition
- Tax withholding and reporting obligations
- Regulatory fees and levies

### 1.3 Governing Principles

- Full compliance with local and international tax regulations
- Transparent tax treatment in all partner agreements
- Accurate and timely tax reporting and remittance
- Adherence to OECD and UN transfer pricing guidelines
- Compliance with BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) requirements

---

## 2. TAX DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 Value Added Tax (VAT) / Goods and Services Tax (GST)

Consumption tax levied on telecommunications services in many jurisdictions. Typically:
- Applied as percentage of service value
- Collected by supplier and remitted to tax authorities
- May be recoverable by business customers (input tax credit)
- Rate varies by jurisdiction (0% - 27%)

### 2.2 Withholding Tax (WHT)

Tax withheld at source on payments to non-resident service providers:
- Applied to cross-border service fees
- Typically 0% - 30% of gross payment
- May be reduced by Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs)
- Withheld by payer and remitted to local tax authority

### 2.3 Universal Service Fund (USF)

Regulatory levy to fund universal telecommunications access:
- Percentage of telecommunications revenue
- Rates typically 1% - 10%
- Varies significantly by country
- May apply to wholesale or retail services

### 2.4 Regulatory Fees and Levies

Government-imposed fees for telecommunications operations:
- Spectrum fees
- Licensing fees
- Numbering resource fees
- Telecommunications regulatory authority levies

### 2.5 Reverse Charge Mechanism

VAT/GST mechanism where recipient (not supplier) accounts for tax:
- Common in B2B cross-border services
- Shifts compliance obligation to service recipient
- Simplifies supplier's VAT obligations
- Requires recipient to be VAT-registered

## 3. TAX TREATMENT BY SERVICE TYPE

### 3.1 Voice Interconnection Services

**Outgoing Voice (Operator → Partner):**
- **VAT/GST:** Generally zero-rated or exempt (export of services)
- **WHT:** Not typically applicable (service provided outside jurisdiction)
- **USF:** May apply based on wholesale revenue in Operator's jurisdiction

**Incoming Voice (Partner → Operator):**
- **VAT/GST:** Reverse charge mechanism may apply (import of services)
- **WHT:** Partner may need to withhold tax on payments to Operator
- **USF:** May apply to wholesale termination fees

### 3.2 SMS and Messaging Services

**Tax Treatment:** Generally follows voice service treatment
- **Cross-Border SMS:** Typically zero-rated for VAT/GST
- **Domestic Termination:** May be subject to local VAT/GST
- **Data Messaging:** May have different classification

### 3.3 Data Roaming Services

**Inbound Roaming (Visitor using Operator's network):**
- **VAT/GST:** May apply based on place of consumption principle
- **WHT:** Not typically applicable
- **Roaming Regulations:** EU and other regions may have specific rules

**Outbound Roaming (Operator's customer on Partner network):**
- **VAT/GST:** Generally charged in home jurisdiction
- **Wholesale Charges:** May be reverse charged

## 4. VAT/GST COMPLIANCE

### 4.1 VAT Registration Requirements

Partners and Operators must:
- Register for VAT/GST where required by revenue thresholds
- Obtain VAT registration numbers in relevant jurisdictions
- Exchange VAT registration details upon agreement execution
- Notify of any changes to VAT status within 30 days

### 4.2 Place of Supply Rules

Determining where telecommunications services are "supplied" for VAT purposes:

**B2B Services (Business-to-Business):**
- General Rule: Supplied where customer is established (reverse charge)
- Exceptions: May vary by jurisdiction

**B2C Services (Business-to-Consumer):**
- General Rule: Supplied where customer uses the service
- Roaming: Special rules apply (usually home country)

### 4.3 VAT Invoice Requirements

Invoices must include:
- Supplier's VAT registration number
- Customer's VAT registration number (if applicable)
- VAT rate applied (or reverse charge statement)
- VAT amount in appropriate currency
- Reference to relevant tax exemption or zero-rating provision

### 4.4 VAT Return Filing

- **Frequency:** Monthly or quarterly (jurisdiction-dependent)
- **Deadline:** Typically 15-30 days after period end
- **Currency:** Local currency (conversion at official rates)
- **Supporting Documentation:** Retention of invoices and records for 5-10 years

## 5. WITHHOLDING TAX COMPLIANCE

### 5.1 Withholding Obligations

**When Withholding Applies:**
- Payments to non-resident companies for services
- No permanent establishment in payer's jurisdiction
- Gross payment exceeds minimum thresholds

**Withholding Tax Rates (Examples):**

| Country | Standard WHT Rate | Typical DTA Rate | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|------------------|-------|
| USA | 30% | 0% - 15% | Treaty-dependent |
| UK | 20% | 0% - 10% | May require clearance |
| India | 10% - 40% | 10% - 15% | Complex categorization |
| UAE | 0% | 0% | No corporate tax (as of 2023) |
| EU Countries | Varies | 0% - 15% | Interest and Royalties Directive may apply |

### 5.2 Double Taxation Agreement (DTA) Relief

To claim reduced WHT rates under DTAs:

**Required Documentation:**
- Certificate of Tax Residency from home country
- Completed tax treaty relief form
- Beneficial ownership declarations
- Purpose of payment documentation

**Process:**
1. Submit documentation 30 days before payment
2. Obtain tax authority approval (if required)
3. Apply reduced rate or claim refund
4. Maintain documentation for audit

### 5.3 Withholding Tax Certificates

Payer must provide WHT certificates showing:
- Gross payment amount
- Tax withheld amount
- Tax authority reference number
- Date of payment to tax authority

**Timeline:** Within 60 days of payment

## 6. UNIVERSAL SERVICE FUND AND REGULATORY FEES

### 6.1 USF Obligations

**Calculation Methods:**
- **Gross Revenue Method:** Percentage of total telecommunications revenue
- **Net Revenue Method:** Percentage after certain deductions
- **Per-Subscriber Method:** Fixed amount per active customer

**Typical Rates by Region:**

| Region | Typical USF Rate | Calculation Basis |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| USA | 34%+ (varies quarterly) | Interstate and international revenue |
| EU Countries | 0% - 3% | Varies by country |
| Africa | 1% - 5% | Gross revenue |
| Asia-Pacific | 0.5% - 6% | Revenue or per-subscriber |
| Latin America | 1% - 5% | Gross telecommunications revenue |

### 6.2 Treatment in Partner Agreements

**Revenue Share Model:**
- Each party responsible for USF in own jurisdiction
- Not passed through in wholesale rates

**Cost-Plus Model:**
- USF may be itemized and billed separately
- Requires agreement clause

### 6.3 Regulatory Fee Types

**Common Regulatory Fees:**
- **Spectrum Fees:** For radio frequency usage
- **Numbering Fees:** For telephone number allocations
- **License Fees:** Annual operating license fees
- **Regulatory Levy:** Funding national regulatory authority

**Allocation in Wholesale Services:**
- Generally borne by the license holder
- Not typically allocated to wholesale partners
- May be factored into pricing strategy

## 7. TRANSFER PRICING REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 Arm's Length Principle

All intercompany and international transactions must be at arm's length:
- Prices comparable to third-party transactions
- Documentation supporting pricing methodology
- Compliance with OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines

### 7.2 Transfer Pricing Methods

**Acceptable Methods:**

1. **Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP):**
   - Direct comparison to third-party prices
   - Preferred method if comparable data available

2. **Cost Plus Method:**
   - Cost basis plus appropriate markup
   - Common for interconnect services

3. **Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM):**
   - Comparing net margins to comparable companies
   - Used when CUP not available

### 7.3 Documentation Requirements

**Master File:** (for multinational groups)
- Organizational structure
- Business description
- Intangible assets
- Financial and tax positions

**Local File:**
- Local entity description
- Controlled transactions
- Comparability analysis
- Transfer pricing method justification

**Country-by-Country Report (CbCR):**
- Revenue, profit, tax, employees by jurisdiction
- Required for groups exceeding €750 million revenue
- Filed annually with tax authorities

### 7.4 Advanced Pricing Agreements (APAs)

Mechanism to obtain advance certainty on transfer pricing:
- Bilateral or unilateral agreements with tax authorities
- Reduces risk of double taxation and disputes
- Typical validity: 3-5 years
- Process: 12-24 months to negotiate

## 8. INVOICING AND TAX REPORTING

### 8.1 Invoice Requirements

All invoices must include:

**Basic Information:**
- Unique invoice number
- Invoice date and payment due date
- Billing period covered
- Operator and Partner legal names and addresses

**Tax Information:**
- VAT/GST registration numbers
- Tax jurisdiction and applicable tax rate
- VAT/GST amount (if applicable)
- Reverse charge statement (if applicable)
- Withholding tax treatment note

**Service Details:**
- Breakdown by service type (Voice, SMS, Data)
- Volume and rates
- Destination breakdown (if applicable)
- Currency and exchange rates used

### 8.2 Tax Reporting Obligations

**Monthly Reporting:**
- VAT/GST returns filed in relevant jurisdictions
- Withholding tax returns and payments

**Quarterly Reporting:**
- USF reports and payments
- Regulatory fee calculations

**Annual Reporting:**
- Transfer pricing documentation
- Country-by-Country Report (if applicable)
- Tax authority audits and information requests

### 8.3 Record Retention

**Minimum Retention Periods:**
- **Invoices and Financial Records:** 7-10 years (varies by jurisdiction)
- **Tax Returns and Supporting Documents:** 7 years minimum
- **Transfer Pricing Documentation:** 6-7 years
- **Correspondence with Tax Authorities:** Indefinitely

## 9. TAX TREATMENT OF SPECIFIC SCENARIOS

### 9.1 Disputed Invoices

**Tax Treatment:**
- VAT/GST accounted for on original invoice amount
- Adjustments via credit notes when dispute resolved
- Withholding tax may need adjustment

**Process:**
1. Original invoice issued with full tax
2. Dispute raised and held in escrow
3. Resolution: Issue credit/debit note with tax adjustment
4. Adjust VAT/GST returns in period of resolution

### 9.2 Refunds and Credits

**Credit Notes Must Include:**
- Reference to original invoice
- Reason for credit
- Adjusted tax amounts
- Date of credit note

**Tax Adjustments:**
- VAT/GST adjustments in period of credit note issuance
- Withholding tax refunds may require separate claims

### 9.3 Currency Exchange

**Exchange Rate Rules:**
- Official central bank rates on date of supply
- Consistent methodology required
- Exchange differences may have tax implications

**Multi-Currency Invoicing:**
- Invoice currency determines initial accounting
- Tax reporting in local currency of each jurisdiction
- Exchange gains/losses tracked separately

### 9.4 Bad Debts

**VAT/GST Relief:**
- Available in most jurisdictions after defined period (e.g., 6-12 months)
- Subject to requirements (e.g., VAT accounted for, debt written off)
- Credit note or adjustment required

## 10. TAX AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

### 10.1 Tax Audit Preparation

**Audit Risk Factors:**
- High volume of cross-border transactions
- Complex intercompany arrangements
- Frequent rate changes
- Large withholding tax claims

**Best Practices:**
- Maintain comprehensive documentation
- Regular internal audits
- Tax advisor review annually
- Prompt response to tax authority inquiries

### 10.2 Partner Cooperation

During tax audits:

**Information Sharing:**
- Partners agree to cooperate with each other's tax audits
- Provide transaction documentation upon request
- Confirm arm's length nature of transactions

**Confidentiality:**
- Information shared only as required by law
- Advance notice to partner before disclosure

### 10.3 Dispute Resolution

**Tax Disputes:**
- Mutual Agreement Procedure (MAP) under tax treaties
- Arbitration clauses (if available)
- Collaboration to minimize double taxation

## 11. TAX OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES

### 11.1 Compliant Tax Planning

**Acceptable Strategies:**
- Utilization of Double Taxation Agreements
- Optimal entity structuring
- Transfer pricing alignment
- Timing of revenue recognition

**Prohibited Strategies:**
- Artificial profit shifting
- Abusive treaty shopping
- Sham transactions
- Mischaracterization of income

### 11.2 Treaty Shopping Prevention

BEPS Action Items compliance:

**Principal Purpose Test (PPT):**
- Benefits denied if principal purpose is tax advantage
- Substance requirements in intermediate jurisdictions

**Limitation on Benefits (LOB):**
- Requirements to qualify for treaty benefits
- Ownership tests
- Activity tests

## 12. JURISDICTIONAL-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### 12.1 European Union

**Key Considerations:**
- Place of supply rules for telecommunications services
- Reverse charge mechanism for B2B services
- VAT Mini One-Stop Shop (MOSS) for B2C services
- Interest and Royalties Directive

### 12.2 United States

**Key Considerations:**
- Federal USF contributions (interstate and international services)
- State and local telecommunications taxes
- 30% withholding tax on non-treaty payments
- IRS Form W-8BEN-E for treaty benefits

### 12.3 Developing Markets

**Key Considerations:**
- Currency controls and remittance restrictions
- Higher withholding tax rates
- Limited tax treaty networks
- Evolving digital services tax regulations

## 13. EMERGING TAX ISSUES

### 13.1 Digital Services Taxes (DST)

Several countries imposing taxes on digital services:
- Typically 2% - 7% on digital service revenues
- Potentially applicable to telecommunications platforms
- May be creditable against corporate income tax

### 13.2 BEPS 2.0 / Pillar Two

Global minimum tax framework:
- 15% minimum effective tax rate
- Applicable to groups with €750 million+ revenue
- Complex calculations and reporting
